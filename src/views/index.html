Power to the people

meet the LDV T60 MAX ute
Simon Webster | Presented by LDV
“If there’s one thing people want in a ute, it’s power”, says Dinesh Chinnappa, General Manager of LDV Australia. And when it comes to power, the new LDV T60 MAX ute is going toe to toe with some of the biggest names out there.

“The T60 MAX has a
bi-turbo diesel engine that puts out 160 kilowatts of power and 500 Newton metres of torque”

“The T60 MAX has a bi-turbo diesel engine that puts out 160 kilowatts of power and 500 Newton metres of torque,” Chinnappa says. “That puts the T60 MAX, in terms of the power and torque, right up with the higher power output units in the market, like the Ford Ranger bi-turbo, or the Toyota Hilux.”

But when it comes to price, there’s no comparison. Rather than looking north of $50,000 for a ute with a high-powered engine, buyers with an ABN can pick up a T60 MAX from $33,990. “This is premium power at a price that hardworking Australians can afford,” Chinnappa says.

Torque of the town

The 2022 T60 MAX is a significant upgrade for the T60, which first hit Australian shores in 2017. Improvements include a revamped look, and more features, including a larger infotainment screen. But the biggest drawcard is undoubtedly the bi-turbocharged engine, designed in-house by LDV’s parent company, SAIC, China’s largest auto manufacturer.

“It allows you to have your adventure weekends, the way you want to. It allows you to go on long, open-road drives, comfortable knowing that you can have overtaking capabilities, knowing that you're not going to be straining the engine the whole time.

“And when you’re in a four-wheel-drive environment, you’ve got the torque to get you up and down those hills and across those ruts. Because that’s what four-wheel-driving needs: it needs torque.”

The T60 MAX comes with a five-year (or 130,000km) warranty and has a five-star ANCAP safety rating. The two variants – the Pro and Luxe – are both available with six-speed manual or eight-speed automatic transmissions, with the auto versions offering the choice of Eco and Power drive modes. Combined fuel consumption is a respectable 9.2 to 9.3 litres per 100km.

A you-brute ute

Adventurers will be pleased to hear that the four-wheel-drive system can be switched between two-wheel-drive, four-wheel-drive high and four-wheel-drive low range. When the terrain gets tricky, you can rely on intelligent real-time torque on demand to supply the grunt as and when it’s needed, with the Luxe also offering rear on-demand diff lock.

Safety features include six airbags, antilock braking system (ABS), hill descent control (HDC), a tyre pressure monitoring system (TPM), fatigue reminder and attention assist. Both models have rear parking sensors and reversing cameras, while the Luxe also boasts a 360-degree panoramic camera and lane departure warning.

And while it might be a beast of a ute in terms of power, the T60 MAX also has plenty of mod cons, from rain-sensing windscreen wipers to auto-dusk-sensing headlights. It’s a comfy ride, with suspension systems that Chinnappa says were designed with Australian conditions in mind, and families certainly won’t object to jumping on board and hitting the road for the weekend. “It’s a five-star car that’s beautifully specified,” Chinnappa says.

With its large grille and sporty alloy wheels, the T60 MAX also stands out in the crowd. “It’s got an aggressive front end,” Chinnappa says. “You notice it on the road. It’s got a good, rugged stance.”

But most importantly, the T60 MAX is great to drive.

“It goes!” Chinnappa says. “And that’s the beauty of it. That’s what makes it so compelling.”
